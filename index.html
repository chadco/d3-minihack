The U.S. earns revenue on natural resources extracted from its lands. This is a major source of revenue for the country and for local municipalities. This site provides an overview of this process, and statistics and data about this industry.

So where do these revenues go?
The answer is it depends. Laws treat revenues from offshore natural resources and onshore natural resources differently. There are set percentages and amounts from each that go certain places every year.

Where do offshore revenues go?

U.S. Treasury

This revenue goes into the U.S. General Fund, which is where money from individual and corporate income taxes go, and accounts for roughly two-thirds of all federal expenditures.

How do natural resources become
Federal revenue?

If there are natural resources under (or even suspected of being under) Federal lands, the U.S. Department of the Interior grants leases to companies to explore for and extract these products to be sold.

In return, part of the sales value is paid to the Federal government for the benefit of the American people. These are called “royalties.” There are other revenues involved, such as rents, fees, and penalties, but by far most of the money comes from these royalties.
